About Us

Project GRACES (Gender and Race in Academic Career Experiences in STEM) is funded by National Science Foundation's 'Early-concept Grants for Exploratory Research' (EAGER) program to provide researchers the opportunity to explore the experience and career trajectories of Black Women faculty in the STEM disciplines.

The goal of working with this sub-population is to obtain feedback and ideas on the approach to adapt the inclusion work and the journey simulations to suit the unique conditions of academic careers, with an emphasis on the career trajectories of Black women in STEM disciplines.

The  Quantitative Studies of Diversity and Inclusion Initiative (QSDI) "aims to leverage novel research methodologies to study diversity & inclusion, with the overall aim of fostering a more inclusive and equitable society."

Our Mission

Our mission is to show experiences of Black women in academic faculty positions in STEM fields. If we can understand what it's like to be a Black woman, we can create a more inclusive and equitable society. Let's make a just society together.
